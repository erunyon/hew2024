:is()

Can I Use: 96%

The :is() pseudo-class function takes a selector list as its argument, and selects any element that can be selected by one of the selectors in that list. This is useful for writing large selectors in a more compact form.

The specificity of the :is() pseudo-class is replaced by the specificity of its most specific argument..

Instead of this


section h1, section h2, section h3,
section h4, section h5, section h6,
article h1, article h2, article h3,
article h4, article h5, article h6,
aside h1, aside h2, aside h3,
aside h4, aside h5, aside h6 {

  color: chartreuse;

}

Do this


:is(section, article, aside) :is(h1, h2, h3, h4, h5, h6) {
    color: chartreuse;
}
